Background

The Tabele Barbatilor ("Census of Men") lists were compiled in 1942 under the Antonescu regime in Romania. They form a systematic record of the Jewish male population during one of the most dangerous periods of Romanian Jewish history, created for the dual purpose of demographic registration and forced labor assignment.

Source & Acquisition: The original documents are held in the Archive and Library of the Federation of Jewish Communities of Romania, in Bucharest (Fond #: III 400 E/1942). Copies were obtained in 2000 by Prof. Ladislau Gyemant, Director of the Mosche Carmilly Institute of Jewish History at the University of Cluj-Napoca, and were purchased from him by The JewishGen Romania Research Division (formerly known as ROM-SIG).

The Three Lists

There are three separate census lists, each covering a distinct age group and purpose:

T1

Born 1881–1892 (Age 50+)

Nationwide. Men above labor age, exempt from forced labor camps due to age. Covers all counties and towns of Romania.

350 pages · 10,574 records
T2

Born 1893–1921 (Ages 21–50)

Bucharest only. Men assigned to labor details, typically working in the businesses of non-Jews.

352 pages · 8,493 records
T3

Born June 1926 – May 1928 (Ages 15–16)

Bucharest only. Boys, many still in school. Not placed in labor details.

71 pages · 1,229 records

This database contains all 20,296 records from T1, T2, and T3 combined.

Research Notes

This database has several features that require context to use effectively. The notes below explain naming conventions, the Romanian education system, profession terminology, and address abbreviations.

In a number of cases individuals are listed by both nicknames or patronyms, as well as the hereditary name adopted in the 1870s, when surnames were legally required in Romania. These are listed in the "Name" column, separated by a slash "/" character. Where a patronym is also given, it is listed separately.

This means a person can be found by either name. For example, an Israel Isaac who was also known as Israel Kleinman will appear under both names, each with a referral to the other.

Note that the Romanian prefix "SIN" means "son of."

Romania's educational system was derived primarily from the French model and does not map directly to the American system. Terms were translated directly to their English meanings. Inconsistencies exist because the census covers men who attended school across many decades and under different occupying powers; rural schools also differed significantly from urban ones.

In the census, level of schooling is preceded by a number and the abbreviation "cl." (short for clases, equivalent to "grade" or "years"). For example: 4 cl. primary = 4 years of primary school; 3 cl. lyceum = 3 years of lyceum (7 total years of schooling).

Key terms used in the database:

  1. Primary / Elementary — Grades 1–4 (rural areas through grade 8)
  2. Gymnasium — Grades 5–8
  3. Lyceum / Secondary — Grades 5–12
  4. Commercial / Lower Commercial / Upper Commercial — Business-type schools (equivalent to US vocational/business schools)
  5. Trade — Vocational/trade school
  6. Graduate — Completed 12 years and passed the bacculareat exam (equivalent to high school diploma)
  7. Doctor of xxx — Attained a PhD
  8. Bachelor of xxx — Attained a bachelor's degree
  9. Polytechnic — Engineering/science university equivalent
  10. xxx Faculty — A discipline-specific school within a university (e.g., facultate de drept = law faculty)
  11. Ciocanul — A specific Romanian-Jewish lyceum
  12. Cultura — A specific set of Romanian-Jewish primary/lyceum schools
  13. Complementary — A school for children with disabilities

Professions are entered exactly as the clerks wrote them in 1942, including any misspellings. It is JewishGen's policy not to correct such entries.

A full Romanian-English occupations translation table is available on the JewishGen InfoFiles pages.

Additional address modifiers:

  • bis — Indicates a second address at the same number (e.g., Vitan 15bis = "the other Vitan 15")
  • et.# — Indicates floor number (e.g., Vitan 25et2 = Vitan 25, 2nd floor)

The following terms appear most frequently in the Assigned Labor column:

  • Proprie / Firma Proprie — Business owner; likely assigned to a self-employed person or small shop owner.
  • C.E.R. / CER / Centrala Evreilor (din Romania) — The Center of the Jews of Romania.
