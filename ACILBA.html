Background

Buenos Aires has the sixth largest Jewish community in the world and the largest in South America. Since 1891, Jews have flocked there — among them a significant wave of Moroccan Sephardic Jews who established their own congregation, known as ACILBA (the Latin Israelite Congregation of Buenos Aires).

The Sephardic marriage records in this collection were provided to JewishGen by Rabbi Shimon Wahnish, the former head of ACILBA, whose congregation consisted largely of Moroccan Jews. The records cover roughly a hundred-year period, from 1920 to 2018, and were provided in the form of original images.

Special thanks are owed to the Wahnish family for their sustained work within ACILBA and for their generosity in contributing these records to JewishGen, making them freely available to researchers worldwide.

About the Database

This database includes 488 marriages from the Moroccan Jewish community in Buenos Aires, spanning 1920 to 2018. Volunteers from the Sephardic Heritage Project harvested the individual data points from the original images.

Each entry captures both the synagogue and civil registry record where available, providing a dual chain of documentation. Many entries also include links directly to the original record images.

Record Terminology

These records draw on two parallel registration systems — the Argentine civil registry and the synagogue's own records — as well as the Hebrew calendar. The terminology sections below explain key terms from each system.

In Argentina, all marriages were required to be registered with the national civil registry (Registro Civil) in addition to any religious ceremony. The following terms appear in the civil registry fields of this database:

Spanish Term Meaning
Acta No. The sequential entry number assigned to the marriage record within the registry volume. Two Acta numbers may appear: one for the synagogue registry and one for the federal civil registry.
Tomo No. Volume number — identifies which bound volume of the civil registry contains the entry.
Circunsc. Short for Circunscripción — the civil registry district. Buenos Aires was divided into numbered districts, and each district maintained its own registry volumes.
Sección / Section A subdivision within a registry volume or district, used to further organize entries.
Federal Acta The entry number in the national (federal) civil registry, distinct from any local or synagogue registry number.

Together, the Tomo, Circunsc, Section, and Acta No. form a precise locator that can be used to request the original civil registry entry from the Argentine national archives (Archivo General de la Nación).

Many entries in this database record both a Gregorian (civil) date and a Hebrew date (Taarich Ivri). The Hebrew calendar is a lunisolar calendar counted from the traditional year of Creation (Anno Mundi, abbreviated AM). The Hebrew year runs approximately from September to September on the Gregorian calendar.

Hebrew Term / Abbreviation Meaning
ה'תש״… / ה'תרצ״… (etc.) Year in the Hebrew calendar (Anno Mundi). To approximate the Gregorian year, subtract 3760 or 3761 depending on the time of year.
Tishrei (תשרי) First month of the Hebrew calendar year, falling in September–October.
Adar / Adar II (אדר) In a Jewish leap year (Shana Me'uberet), a 13th month — Adar II — is added. Records in a leap year will specify which Adar.
ב' (Bet) / ה' (He) etc. Hebrew letters used as numerals to indicate the day of the month.

To convert a Hebrew date to its Gregorian equivalent, use the JewishGen Hebrew/Gregorian Calendar Converter.
