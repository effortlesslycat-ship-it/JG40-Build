Subcarpathia Research Division

Preserving and sharing the histories of Jewish communities from the historical Subcarpathia region.

About the Division

The JewishGen Subcarpathia Research Division preserves and shares the histories of Jewish communities from the historical Subcarpathia region, which includes areas once part of the Austro-Hungarian Empire, Czechoslovakia, Hungary, and the Ukrainian Soviet Socialist Republic. Researchers and descendants can explore an expanding collection of records, transcriptions, and resources documenting life in towns, districts, and provinces, offering rich insights into cultural, social, and family history.

Currently Working On

The Division is in the middle of indexing projects, with new records added to JewishGen as transcription and verification are completed.

Our Team

Lara Diamond

Director

Lara Diamond has been researching her family for 25 years, starting as a middle school student. She has traced all branches of her family multiple generations back in Europe using Russian Empire-era and Austria-Hungarian Empire records. Most of her research is in modern-day Ukraine, with a smattering of Belarus and Poland. As she is an Ashkenazic Jew, she gets to have particular fun with her completely endogamous genome. In addition to serving as Director of the Subcarpathia Research Division, she is president of the Jewish Genealogy Society of Maryland. She also runs multiple district- and town-focused projects to collect documentation to assist all those researching ancestors from common towns. She blogs about DNA and her Eastern European research at Lara’s Jewnealogy.
