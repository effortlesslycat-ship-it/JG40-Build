Research Divisions

Volunteer-led teams expanding JewishGen's collection of records and town histories.

JewishGen Research Divisions focus on expanding our collection of records, adding informational content to our JewishGen Knowledge Base, and encouraging the creation of more KehilaLinks entries for towns that once had a Jewish community.

Austria-Czech

Bohemia and Moravia (now the Czech Republic), plus parts of Austria, especially Vienna.

Belarus

The former Russian Empire gubernii of Grodno, Minsk, Mogilev, Vitebsk, and southern Vilna, now in Belarus.

Bessarabia

The former Russian gubernia of Bessarabia, now in Moldova with parts in Ukraine. Also covers Transnistria, a small region on the left bank of the Dniester.

France

France and French colonies, as well as other French-speaking areas such as Belgium, Luxembourg and Switzerland.

Germany

Germany and German-speaking areas of Alsace, Lorraine, Switzerland, and Poland.

Holocaust

Records and resources documenting Jewish individuals and communities affected by the Shoah across Nazi-occupied Europe.

Hungary

"Greater Hungary" or pre-Trianon Hungary — present-day Hungary and Slovakia, plus territory now within Ukraine, Austria, Romania, Croatia and Serbia.

Latvia

The former Russian Empire gubernias of Courland, southern Livonia (Livland), and western Vitebsk.

Romania

Romania, including Bukovina.

Sephardic

For all researchers of Sephardic genealogy and history.

Sub-Carpathia

Sub-Carpathian Ukraine — parts of the pre-WWI Hungarian megyék of Bereg, Máramaros, Ugocsa and Ung — today Zakarpattia Oblast, Ukraine.

Ukraine

The former Russian Empire gubernias now in Ukraine: Podolia, Volhynia, Kiev, Poltava, Chernigov, Kharkov, Kherson, Taurida and Ekaterinoslav.

USA

Jewish families who came to the United States before, during, and after the larger immigration waves of the 1880s.
