How JOWBR Grows

Every record in JOWBR was contributed by a volunteer — photographed, transcribed, and in many cases translated. The project is coordinated by Nolan Altman, who oversees a global team of indexers and translators working from Hebrew, Cyrillic, and other languages.

JewishGen has also launched an Adopt a Cemetery program, inviting Special Interest Groups, local Jewish genealogy societies, synagogue youth groups, Jewish Federations, and other interested parties to take responsibility for indexing a specific cemetery or landsmanschaft plot.

Please note: JOWBR only accepts data from an entire cemetery or an entire landsmanschaft/organization plot within a larger cemetery. We are unable to accept individual family burial records.

Submitting Data

If you have access to a cemetery or organization plot not yet in JOWBR and are ready to submit, three resources are required. Complete them in order:

  1. 1
    Donor Agreement Required before submitting any data. Grants JewishGen permission to publish and maintain the records online. Complete the Donor Agreement →
  2. 2
    Data Entry Template An Excel spreadsheet with the required fields pre-formatted. Enter one burial record per row. Only fill in the fields for which you have information.
    Download the Excel Template →
  3. 3
    Field Instructions Detailed explanations of each data field — what to include, how to format dates, how to handle Hebrew names, and more. Read the Field Instructions →
